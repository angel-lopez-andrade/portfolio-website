Me + Coding - The Journey To Now

Major in International Business

I studied at Macquarie University in northern Sydney, where I completed a Bachelor of Arts with a major in International Business.

Whilst this was a fun degree and afforded me a lot of room to experiment with electives, I found that the field of study was really to broad to be of much practical use for what companies want in fresh graduates, and a lot of the theories we learned seem to be built for mid-career managers (who actually make the decisions in their companies' international divisions - something a fresh graduate of course wouldn't!).

I found myself drawn to the linguistical side of things - putting a lot of my electives into national languages (amongst other experimentation), and this eventually led me to discover programming languages too.

A Textbook On A Whim

During my degree, I was fortunate enough to be accepted into a student internship at Hitachi in Japan - where I worked in the General Affairs department over the winter.

Hitachi, being quite a tech-savvy firm, got me curious about the IT industry, and one day in a bookstore, I purchased a beginner's Java textbook - pictured, on a whim. This was my first taste of programming.

As I read through it, I became increasingly interested in programming languages. After finishing up two textbooks, I found that Java, as a primarily back-end language, wasn't really scratching that itch for seeing your own work unfold, especially given my beginner level and my lack of project inspiration.

Discovering More Languages

This led me to freeCodeCamp - a community-run, free online bootcamp-style course that I found on the web one day. This course had a focus on front-end development at the start, which had me interested as I was looking for something more visual after a while of Java.

I completed the first two freeCodeCamp modules over the next few months, and got to make a couple of simple page designs and check out a bit of JavaScript - AKA the front-end side of things.

While I learned a lot from freeCodeCamp, I was still somewhat lacking a structure in my study, which I felt would push me to the next level in learning.
